UAE Jurisdictions We Serve

Each UAE jurisdiction has distinct legal traditions and formatting requirements. Our translators understand these differences and produce documents tailored to your specific judicial forum.

Dubai Courts

Dubai Courts operate under UAE Civil Law derived from Egyptian and French legal traditions. All documents must be submitted in Arabic, with MOJ-certified translations accepted for foreign language evidence. Our translators are familiar with Dubai Court filing requirements, document numbering conventions, and the specific terminology used in civil, commercial, labor, and personal status matters.

  • Civil and Commercial Division
  • Labor Court
  • Personal Status Court
  • Court of Appeal and Cassation

DIFC Courts

The DIFC Courts apply common law principles in English. Documents filed in Arabic require certified English translations, and vice versa. We understand DIFC procedural rules, the Court of First Instance filing conventions, and the specific requirements for commercial disputes involving DIFC-registered entities, financial services claims, and cross-border enforcement matters.

  • Court of First Instance
  • Small Claims Tribunal
  • Court of Appeal
  • Enforcement Proceedings

ADGM Courts

Abu Dhabi Global Market Courts operate under English common law, with proceedings conducted primarily in English. For documents originating in other languages, certified translations are required. We are experienced with ADGM Court procedures, including digital filing requirements, witness statement formats, and the specific conventions for financial and commercial disputes.

  • Court of First Instance
  • Arbitration Centre
  • Court of Appeal
  • Employment Tribunal

Common Legal Translation Scenarios

Understanding when and why you need certified legal translation helps ensure you have the right documents ready for your specific legal requirements.

Court Filings and Litigation

Dubai Courts require all documents to be submitted in Arabic. If your original contract, evidence, or correspondence is in another language, certified translation is mandatory before filing. This applies to statements of claim, defense submissions, witness statements, and documentary evidence. We understand court filing deadlines and can expedite translations to meet your submission schedule.

For DIFC and ADGM Courts, the reverse is often true. Arabic documents require English translation for filing in these common law jurisdictions. We ensure translations comply with each court's procedural rules and formatting expectations.

Company Formation and Licensing

Establishing a business in the UAE requires translated corporate documents including Memoranda of Association, shareholder agreements, board resolutions, and parent company documentation. DED, DMCC, JAFZA, and other licensing authorities have specific requirements for translated documents. We ensure your corporate documentation meets these requirements and is formatted for efficient processing.

For branch office registrations, translated parent company documents from your home jurisdiction must be certified and, often, attested through the embassy chain before submission to UAE authorities.

Arbitration and International Disputes

International arbitration proceedings often involve documents in multiple languages. Whether you are filing before DIAC, DIFC-LCIA, or ICC, accurate translation of contracts, correspondence, and expert reports is essential. Arbitration awards themselves require certified translation for enforcement in other jurisdictions under the New York Convention.

We handle the translation of complex technical and financial documents that frequently arise in construction arbitration, shipping disputes, and investment treaty claims, ensuring terminology consistency across large document sets.

Real Estate Transactions

Property transactions in Dubai involve numerous legal documents requiring translation: Sale and Purchase Agreements (SPAs), title deeds, mortgage agreements, and Powers of Attorney for property transactions. DLD registration requires Arabic documentation, making certified translation essential for foreign buyers and sellers.

For off-plan purchases, we translate developer contracts and payment schedules. For disputes before the Rental Dispute Center (RDC), we provide court-ready translations of tenancy contracts, Ejari certificates, and correspondence with landlords or tenants.

Legal Translation FAQ

Common questions about legal document translation in the UAE.

Is MOJ certification required for all court submissions?
Yes. Dubai Courts, Abu Dhabi Courts, and all UAE federal courts require translations to be certified by a Ministry of Justice licensed translator. This certification confirms that the translation is accurate and complete. Uncertified translations will be rejected by the court registry. For DIFC and ADGM Courts, while the certification requirements differ slightly, having MOJ certification is generally accepted and recommended.
How long does legal document translation take?
Standard turnaround for legal documents is 24-72 hours depending on complexity and page count. A simple Power of Attorney may be completed within 24 hours, while a 50-page commercial contract with technical schedules may require 3-5 business days. For court filing deadlines, we offer express services with same-day delivery. Contact us with your deadline and we will confirm feasibility before you commit.
Can you translate contracts that are being negotiated?
Yes. We frequently translate draft contracts during negotiation phases to enable bilingual review by all parties. For active negotiations with multiple versions, we can track changes and provide updated translations efficiently. Once the contract is finalized and signed, we produce the final certified version for execution or filing purposes.
Do you provide confidentiality agreements?
Yes. We understand that legal documents often contain sensitive commercial or personal information. We maintain strict confidentiality for all documents we handle. For particularly sensitive matters such as M&A transactions, litigation strategy documents, or trade secret materials, we can execute a formal Non-Disclosure Agreement before commencing work.
Can you deliver translations directly to court?
Yes. For urgent court filings, we can arrange courier delivery directly to Dubai Courts, DIFC Courts, or your legal representative's office. This is particularly useful when you are working against a filing deadline and need to minimize transit time. Let us know your filing deadline and location, and we will coordinate delivery accordingly.
