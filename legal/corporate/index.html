Corporate Translation FAQ

‚ùì Should I translate before or after signing? ‚ñº

Best practice: Translate unsigned draft, then sign both versions simultaneously.

Why: Ensures both Arabic and English versions are identical and signed on same date. If you translate after signing, dates won't match and courts may question authenticity.

Alternative: If already signed, we can translate and add translator's note explaining translation date differs from execution date.

‚ùì Which language governs if there's a conflict? ‚ñº

UAE courts: Typically give precedence to Arabic version if conflict exists.

DIFC/ADGM: English version may govern if contract specifies.

Our approach: We ensure both versions say exactly the same thing, eliminating conflict risk. If ambiguity exists in original, we flag it for clarification before translation.

‚ùì Do I need notarization after translation? ‚ñº

Depends on use:

  • Internal use: MOJ translation usually sufficient
  • Court filing: May need notary public attestation
  • Government submission: Check specific authority requirements
  • International use: May need MOFA attestation

We verify requirements before starting and coordinate notarization if needed.

‚ùì Can you translate amendments and addendums? ‚ñº

Yes. We maintain terminology consistency with original contract:

  • Amendment tracking: Clear reference to original contract
  • Consistent terms: Same legal terms as original translation
  • Cross-references: Clause references updated correctly
  • Version control: Amendment number and date clearly marked

Send original translated contract with amendment for consistency check.

‚ùì What if contract has technical or industry-specific terms? ‚ñº

We handle specialized contracts:

  • Technical terms: Verified with industry glossaries
  • Financial terms: Standard banking/finance terminology
  • Real estate: RERA-compliant property terms
  • IT/Software: Technology licensing terms

For highly specialized contracts, we may consult subject matter expert to ensure accuracy.
