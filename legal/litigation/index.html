Litigation Translation FAQ

‚ùì Will Dubai Courts accept your translations? ‚ñº

Yes. All litigation translations are executed by UAE Ministry of Justice-licensed legal translators and carry the official MOJ stamp.

Accepted by:

  • Dubai Courts: All levels (First Instance, Appeal, Cassation)
  • DIFC Courts: Small Claims, Court of First Instance, Court of Appeal
  • ADGM Courts: Court of First Instance, Court of Appeal
  • Arbitration centers: DIAC, DIFC-LCIA, ADCCAC, ICC
  • Other emirates: Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, and all UAE courts

Court formatting: We follow each court's specific formatting requirements. Dubai Courts have different standards than DIFC, which we apply automatically.

‚ùì How do you handle exhibit numbering and cross-references? ‚ñº

Exhibit tracking system: We maintain a master exhibit list for your case.

Process:

  • Initial numbering: We number exhibits sequentially (Exhibit 1, 2, 3, etc.)
  • Cross-reference tracking: Every reference to an exhibit in pleadings is verified
  • Paragraph references: References to specific paragraphs in other documents are maintained
  • Bundle organization: Exhibits organized with cover sheet and index

Example: If your pleading references "Exhibit 5, paragraph 12," we ensure Exhibit 5 exists, is numbered correctly, and paragraph 12 is accurately referenced in both Arabic and English.

üí° Best Practice: Send all case documents together so we can create a complete exhibit index and maintain consistent numbering throughout your case.

Process:

‚ùì Can you translate foreign court judgments for enforcement in UAE? ‚ñº

Yes. We regularly translate foreign judgments for enforcement proceedings in UAE courts.

Common scenarios:

  • UK/US judgments: For enforcement in Dubai Courts or DIFC
  • GCC judgments: For recognition under GCC enforcement treaty
  • European judgments: For enforcement proceedings
  • Arbitration awards: For enforcement under New York Convention

Additional requirements:

  • Apostille/attestation: Foreign judgment usually needs apostille or embassy attestation before translation
  • Certified copy: Court-certified copy of judgment required
  • Translation certification: MOJ stamp applied after translation
  • Notarization: May need notary attestation depending on enforcement court

We verify specific requirements for your enforcement jurisdiction before starting.
