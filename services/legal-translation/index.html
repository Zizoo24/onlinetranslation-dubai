COURT-READY LEGAL TRANSLATION

What Legal Translation in the UAE Actually Involves

Legal translation here is not a creative writing exercise. It is a regulated activity that sits between language and procedure. A document can be linguistically perfect and still be rejected at Dubai Courts, DLD, or a bank because a stamp, format, or authority requirement was missed.

Under UAE law and court practice, Arabic is the governing language for most official procedures. That means contracts, judgments, powers of attorney, company documents, and many other records must either be drafted in Arabic or accompanied by a Ministry of Justice certified Arabic translation.

In parallel, the UAE hosts multiple forums — Dubai Courts, DIFC Courts, ADGM, free zones, regulators, and foreign consulates — each with its own habits, templates, and tolerances. A phrase that is acceptable in a bilingual DIFC contract may be too vague for a Dubai Courts dispute. A “generic” translation can cause confusion, delay, or in serious cases, be seen as misrepresentation.

OnlineTranslation.ae operates as a managed document-clearing desk. We coordinate MOJ-licensed legal translators and specialist partners, keep one eye on wording and one eye on where the papers are going, and help you avoid unnecessary trips, resubmissions, and appointment delays.

What You Actually Receive

  • Draft translations for review in PDF or Word (when appropriate).
  • Final printed sets with MOJ translator stamp, signature, and license details on each relevant page.
  • Clear indication of source and target languages (e.g. English ⇄ Arabic), and number of pages translated.
  • Optional soft copies of the stamped versions for your records and future filings.

Who Signs and Stamps

  • A named MOJ-licensed legal translator, operating under an approved license in the UAE.
  • Where needed, an affiliated legal translation office (e.g. Arkan Legal Translation) provides the trade license and office stamp.
  • For complex matters, specialist lawyers and subject-matter experts may be consulted before finalizing terminology.

Why a Managed Desk Helps

  • You do not have to learn which authority accepts which format or stamp.
  • We flag missing attestations, expired documents, or incomplete bundles before you spend money on translation that cannot be used.
  • For urgent cases, we coordinate timelines with your filing deadlines so you can plan appointments with realistic buffers.

PRE-CHECK BEFORE TRANSLATION

Quick Checks Before You Spend Money on Translation

A few minutes of checking can prevent wasted translation fees and rescheduled appointments. If any of the points below are unclear, you can simply send the documents as they are and we will advise whether it is even worth translating yet.

Vital Records & Certificates

  • Is the certificate complete, with no corners or stamps cut off in the scan?
  • Has it been attested where needed (home country, embassy, MOFA)?
  • Are names written exactly as in the passport (including middle names)?
  • Is the document still valid for its purpose (some bodies reject older versions)?
  • Do you know which authority will receive it first (court, consulate, employer, etc.)?

Legal, Corporate & Court Files

  • Is the set complete (all pages, annexes, and schedules included)?
  • Are there handwritten amendments that must be captured in the translation?
  • Have you already filed any bilingual versions that we should match for consistency?
  • Are there hard deadlines (hearing dates, bank cut-offs, completion dates)?
  • Do different authorities see different subsets of the same documents?

Technical, Medical & Digital Content

  • Are you sending final versions, not drafts that will change again next week?
  • Do you have reference glossaries or prior translations we should follow?
  • Are measurement units, dosages, or parameters clearly readable in the scans?
  • Is any of the content subject to additional approvals (compliance, medical, regulatory)?
  • Will the translated text be re-inserted into software, labels, or interfaces?

If you are unsure about any of this, you can still proceed. Part of our job as a managed desk is to tell you when translation should wait until a document is attested, reissued, or finalised.

QUESTIONS WE HEAR OFTEN

Legal Translation FAQ

A few plain-language answers to common questions about certified legal translation in the UAE. If something is not covered here, you can ask directly on WhatsApp.

Are your translations accepted by UAE authorities and courts?

Translations are carried out by MOJ-licensed legal translators whose stamps and license details are recognised by UAE courts and government bodies. For each assignment, we confirm where the papers are going — courts, ministries, banks, developers, or consulates — and prepare the translations accordingly. If a particular authority has unusual requirements, we tell you in advance.

How fast can I realistically expect delivery?

For standard certificates and short legal documents, 2–3 working days is typical. Express matters tied to court hearings, bank appointments, or visa deadlines can often be handled within 24–48 hours, subject to length and complexity. Very long contracts, handwritten bundles, or multi-language files are quoted on a case-by-case basis.

Can I just send photos from my phone?

Yes. Clear mobile photos are fine for quoting and often for translation, as long as all corners and stamps are visible. If the quality is borderline for court or ministry use, we will say so and recommend rescanning or using a document scanner app before final stamping.

Which languages do you cover besides Arabic and English?

The core flow is Arabic ⇄ English, but we regularly handle matters involving French, German, Russian, Hindi/Urdu, and several other languages through trusted partners. For less common combinations, we may route the job through two steps (for example, French ⇄ English ⇄ Arabic) and explain the implications for cost and timing.

What happens if an error is found after delivery?

If there is a genuine error attributable to the translation (for example, a typo in a name or number, or an omission), we arrange a corrected version and fresh stamped pages without additional translation fees. Where the underlying document itself changes, or a new authority with different rules becomes involved, we will treat that as a new assignment and quote accordingly.

If your situation does not fit neatly into any of the above, you can still reach out. A short WhatsApp message with a few photos is usually enough for us to tell you what is realistic, what is urgent, and what can wait.
