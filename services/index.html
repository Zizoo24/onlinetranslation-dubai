Before You Translate

Quick Checks That Save You a Second Trip

Many rejected applications in the UAE are not rejected because of the translation. They are rejected because of missing stamps, expired documents, or unclear scans. Part of the job here is to catch those issues before you spend money on a service that will not be accepted.

For Vital Records & Personal Certificates

  • Is the certificate fully visible in the scan, including all corners and seals?
  • Has it been attested in the country of issue where required?
  • Does it carry the Ministry of Foreign Affairs stamp when the authority expects it?
  • Do all family members‚Äô names match their passports exactly?

For Legal, Corporate & Court Files

  • Are you working on the latest signed version of the document?
  • Do you know which court, regulator, or bank will receive it?
  • Is there a clear deadline and, if so, is it realistic for translation and review?
  • Are exhibits and annexes organised in a way that can be mirrored in Arabic/English?

For Technical, Medical & Digital Content

  • Is there a reference version or glossary that should be followed?
  • Will non-specialists need to sign or approve the document after translation?
  • Does any part of the text double as a legal disclaimer or consent?

If you are unsure about any of these points, you can simply send what you have. Part of the role here is to help you decide whether the document is ready for translation or needs one more step first.

FAQ

Questions That Come Up Again and Again

New clients tend to ask the same practical questions. Here are a few of them, answered in plain language.

Are your translations accepted by UAE authorities?

Translations are produced through UAE Ministry of Justice-licensed legal translators and are routinely used with Dubai Courts, other onshore courts, DIFC, ADGM, free zones, immigration authorities, and banks. If a specific authority has unusual formatting or attestation requirements, that is discussed with you before work begins.

How fast can I realistically expect delivery?

Short certificates and simple letters can often be handled within the same working day once everything is clear. Larger bundles and complex contracts need more time. Rather than quoting generic ‚Äúone hour‚Äù promises, you receive a realistic window based on your actual file and deadline.

Can I just send photos from my phone?

Yes, as long as the scans are clear, corners are visible, and no part of the stamp or text is cut off. If an image is too dark or cropped, you will be asked to resend it before work starts so that the final translation is reliable.

Which languages do you cover besides Arabic and English?

The core pairing is Arabic ‚Üî English. For other languages ‚Äì such as Russian, French, German, Italian, Spanish, Turkish, Hindi, Urdu, Farsi, and Chinese ‚Äì trusted collaborators are used, especially for supporting material. Where a translation needs to be relied upon in court or before an authority, the work is coordinated so that legal review is built in.

How are errors handled if something slips through?

The goal is always to catch issues before they leave the desk. That said, no process is perfect. If a genuine error is found in a delivered translation, it is corrected and reissued as quickly as possible, and replacement physical copies are arranged where needed. The objective is not to pretend mistakes never happen, but to own them and fix them without drama.
