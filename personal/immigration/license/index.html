Converting your foreign driving license to a UAE license is one of the essential steps in settling into life in Dubai. The process seems straightforward, but a surprising number of applications face delays or rejection due to documentation issues. The most common problem is submitting an unofficial or incomplete translation of the original license, which RTA systems flag immediately.

We provide MOJ-certified Arabic translations of driving licenses from any country, formatted specifically for RTA submission. Our translation includes all the details RTA requires: your personal information exactly as it should appear on your Emirates ID, the vehicle categories you are licensed to drive, the issue and expiry dates, and any restrictions or endorsements noted on your original license. When you submit our translation at the typing center, the application proceeds without the delays that plague incomplete documentation.

Beyond the translation itself, we understand the broader RTA process and can guide you through what comes next. Whether your country is on the approved list for automatic conversion or you need to complete the driving test route, we explain the steps clearly so you know exactly what to expect before you visit the RTA service center.

Step-by-Step Translation Process

From the moment you contact us to the moment you have your translated license in hand, here is exactly what happens. We designed this process to be as efficient as possible while maintaining the accuracy that RTA requires.

Send us a clear photograph or scan of your driving license via WhatsApp. We need both sides if your license has information on the back, which is common for European-format licenses that include vehicle categories on the reverse. Within minutes, we review the image to confirm:

  • Legibility: All text must be readable. Worn or faded licenses may need special handling.
  • Validity: We check that your license has not expired. Expired licenses cannot be converted.
  • Country status: We confirm whether your issuing country is on the approved list for direct conversion.
  • Category coverage: We identify which vehicle categories appear on your license so these can be accurately translated.

If there are any issues, we explain them immediately rather than discovering problems later in the process. This pre-validation is complimentary and takes only a few minutes.

2

Professional Legal Translation

Once you confirm you wish to proceed, our MOJ-licensed translators begin work on your license. Driving licenses contain specific terminology that must be translated accurately for RTA acceptance. This includes:

  • Personal details: Your name is transliterated into Arabic following the same conventions used on your Emirates ID. If there is a discrepancy between your license name and your passport name, we flag this for you because RTA requires consistency.
  • Vehicle categories: Categories like A (motorcycle), B (light vehicle), C (heavy vehicle), D (bus), and others are translated with the correct Arabic equivalents that RTA recognizes.
  • Dates: Issue date, expiry date, and any validity periods are converted to the format RTA systems expect.
  • Restrictions and endorsements: Any conditions on your license, such as "corrective lenses required" or "automatic transmission only," are included in the translation.
  • Issuing authority: The name of the authority that issued your original license is translated for the official record.

Our translators are familiar with license formats from dozens of countries and know exactly which details RTA requires. This expertise prevents the common errors that cause applications to be returned for correction.

3

Draft Review and Name Verification

Before we finalize and stamp your translation, we send you a draft via WhatsApp for your review. This step is critical because RTA requires that the name on your translated license matches exactly with your Emirates ID. Pay special attention to:

  • Date formats: Verify that issue and expiry dates are correctly captured.
  • Vehicle categories: Confirm all the categories on your original license are reflected in the translation.
  • Restrictions: If your original license has any restrictions, verify they appear correctly.

Most clients approve the draft within minutes. If any corrections are needed, we make them immediately at no additional charge. Only after your explicit approval do we proceed to final printing and official stamping.

MODULE C

After-Care Guide: RTA File Opening and License Conversion

Once you have your translated license, here is what comes next. The exact process varies depending on whether your country is on the approved list. We explain both pathways so you know exactly what to expect.

RTA File Opening Process

The first step at RTA is opening a "traffic file" in your name. This is the record that will eventually be linked to your UAE driving license. Here is the typical file opening procedure:

  1. Visit an RTA-authorized typing center. These are located throughout Dubai. Major centers are at Umm Ramool, Al Qusais, Al Awir, and Deira. Each emirate has its own RTA or traffic department for residents of that emirate.
  2. Submit your documents. Present your passport with residence visa, Emirates ID, translated driving license, eye test certificate, and photographs. The typing center staff will verify everything.
  3. Complete the application form. The typing center fills in the official RTA application with your details. Review it carefully before signing.
  4. Pay the file opening fees. RTA fees apply for file opening and processing. Fees vary by service type and are subject to change.
  5. Receive your file number. This number tracks your application through the RTA system. Keep it safe for all future correspondence.

Eye Test and Medical Requirements

Every license applicant must pass an eye test. This is straightforward and can be done at any RTA-approved optician across Dubai.

  1. Locate an approved optician. Major chains like Magrabi, Yateem, and Al Jaber are all approved. Many smaller opticians also offer RTA eye tests. Look for the RTA approval sign.
  2. Bring your Emirates ID. The optician needs this to issue the certificate in the correct format.
  3. Wear corrective lenses if needed. If you normally wear glasses or contact lenses for driving, wear them during the test. The certificate will note this requirement.
  4. Complete the test. The test checks visual acuity (reading letters at a distance) and color vision. It takes about 10 minutes.
  5. Receive your certificate. The certificate is issued immediately and links directly to your Emirates ID in RTA systems.

The eye test certificate is typically valid for 3-6 months. Complete it shortly before your RTA file opening to ensure it remains valid throughout the process.

Approved Countries: Direct Conversion Path

If your country is on the RTA approved list, you can convert your license without taking a driving test. After file opening with your translated license and eye test, the process is:

  1. File verification. RTA verifies your foreign license authenticity. This may involve contacting the issuing authority in some cases.
  2. Processing period. Standard processing takes a few working days. Express processing is available for faster service.
  3. License issuance. Once approved, your UAE driving license is printed and either collected at the service center or delivered to you.
  4. Categories transferred. The vehicle categories from your original license are reflected on your new UAE license, subject to RTA category equivalencies.

The entire process from file opening to receiving your UAE license typically takes 3-7 working days for approved country nationals.

Non-Approved Countries: Testing Path

If your country is not on the approved list, you must complete driving lessons and pass the RTA driving test. The translated license still helps:

  1. File opening with foreign license. Your translated license is submitted as part of file opening. Holding a foreign license may reduce required training hours.
  2. Theory test. Complete the RTA theory test covering UAE traffic rules and regulations. Study materials are available in multiple languages.
  3. Driving lessons. Register with an RTA-approved driving school. The number of required lessons depends on your experience level.
  4. Practical test. Once your instructor certifies you as ready, schedule and pass the RTA road test.
  5. License issuance. Upon passing the practical test, your UAE driving license is issued.

Some driving schools offer assessment tests that can reduce required training hours for experienced drivers. The translated license proves your prior driving experience.

MODULE E

Frequently Asked Questions

These are the questions we answer most often about driving license translation and RTA conversion. If your situation is not covered here, please reach out directly for personalized guidance.

Is my country on the RTA approved list for direct license conversion?

The UAE maintains a list of countries whose driving licenses can be converted directly to a UAE license without requiring a driving test. This list includes most Western European countries (UK, Germany, France, Italy, Spain, Netherlands, Belgium, Austria, Switzerland, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Ireland, Portugal, Greece, and others), the United States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, South Africa, Turkey, and all GCC countries (Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, Oman).

If your country is on this list, the conversion process involves submitting your translated license, passing an eye test, and completing the administrative requirements. No driving test is needed. If your country is not on the list, you will need to complete driving lessons at an RTA-approved school and pass the practical driving test, though your prior experience as a licensed driver may reduce required training hours.

The approved countries list is subject to change. During our initial document review, we confirm your country's current status so you know exactly which pathway applies to your situation.

What license categories can be converted to a UAE license?

RTA can convert multiple license categories, not just the standard car license. Common categories include:

  • Light motor vehicle (car): The most common category, equivalent to Category B in European licenses.
  • Motorcycle: Equivalent to Category A. Some countries have tiered motorcycle categories based on engine size, which RTA may consolidate.
  • Light truck: Vehicles up to a certain weight limit.
  • Heavy truck: Commercial goods vehicles above the light truck threshold.
  • Light bus: Passenger vehicles under a certain capacity.
  • Heavy bus: Large passenger vehicles for commercial transport.

If your original license includes multiple categories, each can typically be converted, though commercial vehicle categories may require additional documentation or verification. We translate all category designations accurately so the RTA can issue your UAE license with the appropriate categories.

Note that some categories on your original license may not have direct UAE equivalents, and RTA makes the final determination on which categories appear on your UAE license.

How long is my translated driving license valid for RTA submission?

The MOJ-certified translation itself does not have a fixed expiry date. Unlike some documents that require recent translation, driving license translations remain valid as long as the underlying original license remains valid. The key date is the expiry date on your original driving license, not the translation date.

However, there are practical considerations:

  • Original license validity: Your foreign license must be valid at the time of conversion. If it expires before you complete the RTA process, you may need to renew it in your home country and obtain a new translation.
  • Processing time: We recommend beginning the conversion process while your license has at least 6 months of validity remaining, giving adequate time for any unexpected delays.
  • System updates: If significant time passes between translation and submission, RTA may request a fresh translation to ensure data accuracy, though this is uncommon.

For the smoothest experience, complete your translation and RTA submission in the same general timeframe rather than translating far in advance.

Do I need to translate my license if it is already in English?

Yes. RTA requires an Arabic translation of your driving license regardless of the original language. This applies to licenses from the UK, USA, Australia, Canada, Ireland, and all other English-speaking countries. The requirement exists because:

  • Official record keeping: UAE government systems maintain records in Arabic, and your file needs an Arabic version for the official record.
  • Format standardization: Driving licenses vary significantly in format between countries. The translation presents all information in a consistent format that RTA systems expect.
  • Verification: The MOJ-certified translation provides a verified record of your license details, certified by a licensed legal translator.

This is a standard requirement that applies to all foreign licenses. The translation process is the same regardless of original language, and we are familiar with English license formats from all issuing countries.

Can I convert a license if I am on a visit visa?

No. RTA license conversion is only available to UAE residents with a valid residence visa. You must have your Emirates ID and residence visa stamped in your passport before you can apply for a UAE driving license through conversion or testing.

If you are currently on a visit visa, you have options for legal driving in the UAE:

  • International Driving Permit (IDP): If you have an IDP issued by your home country along with your valid foreign license, you can drive in the UAE for short-term purposes.
  • Foreign license with IDP: Many car rental companies accept a valid foreign license with IDP for tourist rentals.
  • GCC license exception: If you hold a valid driving license from another GCC country, you may be able to drive in the UAE with that license for an extended period.

Once you obtain your residence visa and Emirates ID, you can begin the license conversion process. The translated license will be ready for submission whenever your residency is confirmed.

What documents do I need for RTA file opening?

For RTA file opening and license conversion, you need to present the following at the typing center:

  • Original foreign driving license: Must be valid and not suspended. Bring the physical original, not a photocopy.
  • MOJ-certified Arabic translation: The translation we provide, stamped and signed by the licensed translator.
  • Passport: With your valid UAE residence visa stamped inside.
  • Emirates ID: Original card. Ensure it is active and not expired.
  • Passport-size photographs: Typically 4-6 photos with white background. Some centers take photos on-site for a fee.
  • Eye test certificate: From an RTA-approved optician. Complete this before your typing center visit.

Additional requirements may apply in specific cases:

  • No Objection Certificate (NOC): Some nationalities or employment visa holders may need an NOC from their sponsor. Check with your employer or PRO.
  • Company letter: If applying under a company visa, some typing centers request a letter confirming your employment.

We can provide guidance on the complete document set based on your specific visa type and nationality during our initial consultation.

Where can I get the eye test for RTA license conversion?

Eye tests for RTA driving license applications can be conducted at any RTA-approved optician. These are widely available across Dubai and other emirates. Look for the RTA approval sign or ask the optician if they provide RTA eye tests. Approved opticians include:

  • Major optical chains: Magrabi Optical, Yateem Optician, Al Jaber Optical, Grand Optics, Rivoli Vision, and similar established chains.
  • Mall-based opticians: Many opticians in Dubai Mall, Mall of the Emirates, and other major shopping centers are RTA-approved.
  • Independent opticians: Numerous smaller opticians throughout Dubai offer RTA eye tests. Look for the approval certificate displayed in their store.
  • RTA service centers: Some RTA service centers have on-site eye testing facilities for convenience.

The test itself is simple and quick, typically taking about 10 minutes. It checks visual acuity (reading letters at a distance) and color vision. If you wear glasses or contact lenses for driving, bring them and wear them during the test. The certificate will note whether corrective lenses are required, and this condition will appear on your UAE license.

The eye test certificate is typically valid for 3-6 months. We recommend completing it shortly before your planned RTA file opening visit.

My license has expired. Can I still convert it?

An expired license cannot be converted through the standard RTA process. The foreign license must be valid at the time of conversion. However, you have options depending on your situation:

  • Renew in your home country: If possible, renew your license through your home country's licensing authority. Many countries allow renewal while abroad, either through embassy services or online portals. Once renewed, you can proceed with translation and conversion.
  • Consider the driving test route: If renewal is not practical, you may apply for a UAE license through the standard testing route (driving lessons and practical test). Your driving history still counts as experience even if the license has expired.
  • Check embassy services: Some embassies can facilitate license renewal or issue driving certificates that may be acceptable to RTA. This varies by country.

If your license expired recently and by a small margin, it may still be worth checking with RTA as policies can vary. We can translate the license and confirm with RTA what options exist for your specific case.

How long does the entire license conversion process take?

For residents from approved countries, the total timeline from starting the translation to holding your UAE license is typically 5-10 working days. Here is the breakdown:

  • Translation: 6-24 hours depending on service tier (our part).
  • Eye test: Same day, completed before or after translation.
  • File opening at typing center: 1-2 hours visit, completed same day as submission.
  • RTA processing: 3-7 working days for verification and approval.
  • License collection or delivery: Available once processing is complete.

For residents from non-approved countries who need to complete driving lessons and testing, the timeline extends significantly based on driving school scheduling and test availability. The translation and file opening portion remains the same, but the training and testing phase can take several weeks.

We can help you plan the process efficiently. If you have a deadline, such as an expiring original license or upcoming travel that requires a UAE license, let us know so we can recommend the appropriate service tier and timing.
