The Complete Attestation Chain for UAE Acceptance

For your translated PCC to be accepted by UAE government authorities, the original document typically needs to pass through a specific attestation chain. While exact requirements vary by country of origin and the UAE authority reviewing your documents, the general pattern is as follows:

Step 1: Home Country Authentication

  • Notarization by a public notary or solicitor (where applicable)
  • Authentication by the relevant government department
  • Apostille stamp (for Hague Convention countries)
  • Non-apostille countries require additional steps

Step 2: Foreign Affairs Attestation

  • Attestation by your home country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  • This confirms the document's authenticity for international use
  • Processing times vary from same-day to several weeks
  • Some countries require specific application forms

Step 3: UAE Embassy Attestation

  • Attestation by the UAE Embassy or Consulate in your home country
  • This step must be completed before arriving in the UAE
  • Embassy fees and processing times vary by location
  • Some embassies require appointments

Step 4: UAE MOFA/MOFAIC Attestation

  • Final attestation by UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  • Can be done online through MOFAIC app
  • Usually completed within 24-48 hours
  • This step is required before translation for some authorities

Note: The attestation chain must typically be completed before translation for the document to be officially accepted. The translation then references all attestation stamps and stickers present on the document. We review your document's attestation status during our initial check and advise you on next steps specific to your situation.

After-Care Guide: What Happens After Your PCC is Translated

Translation is one step in a larger process. Here is what typically comes next for Golden Visa applicants, employment visa seekers, and others using their translated PCC for UAE purposes.

Golden Visa Applications via ICP

If you are applying for a Golden Visa through ICP (Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Port Security), the translated PCC is typically submitted as part of your initial application package. The process generally follows these steps:

  1. Prepare your complete document set. This includes passport copies, passport-size photos, proof of eligibility (property ownership, investment certificates, employment contracts for specialized talent categories, or educational credentials for students), and the translated PCC.
  2. Submit through the ICP app or authorized typing center. Golden Visa applications can be submitted online through the ICP mobile app or website. Some applicants prefer to use authorized typing centers for assistance.
  3. Complete medical examination and biometrics. You will need to undergo a medical fitness test at an approved center and have your biometrics (fingerprints and photo) captured for Emirates ID.
  4. Receive your Golden Visa. Upon approval, the 10-year Golden Visa is stamped in your passport, and you receive your Emirates ID separately.

Validity window reminder: Ensure your PCC was issued recently enough that it remains valid throughout the application processing period, which can take 2-4 weeks depending on the category.

Employment Visa Processing via GDRFA

For employment visas, your employer or PRO typically handles the application through GDRFA (General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs). Your translated PCC is part of the required documentation. The typical flow is:

  1. Entry permit issuance. Your employer applies for an entry permit, which allows you to enter the UAE for employment purposes.
  2. Status change (if already in UAE). If you are already in the UAE on a visit visa, a status change may be required before proceeding.
  3. Medical and Emirates ID. Complete the medical fitness test and biometrics for Emirates ID.
  4. Visa stamping. Submit all documents including the translated PCC for final visa stamping in your passport.

Employer requirements vary: Some employers request the PCC before offering the position, while others only require it during the formal visa process. Confirm with your employer or their PRO which documents they need and when.

PCC Validity and Renewal Considerations

Police Clearance Certificates have limited validity, and you may need to obtain and translate a new one in several scenarios:

  1. Application delays. If your visa application is delayed and your PCC expires during processing, you may need a fresh one. Monitor your PCC's issue date carefully.
  2. Visa renewal. Some visa categories require a new PCC upon renewal, particularly if significant time has passed or if you have traveled extensively since the last application.
  3. Family sponsorship. When sponsoring family members, they may each need their own PCCs from every country where they have resided.
  4. Employer change. Changing employers within the UAE may require a new PCC if significant time has elapsed since your last application.
  5. Citizenship applications. For those eligible for UAE citizenship, a comprehensive PCC covering your entire residence history may be required.

We keep records of your previous translations and can reference them when you need updates or new translations of renewed PCCs.

Multiple PCCs for Multi-Country Residents

If you have lived in multiple countries during the lookback period (typically 5-10 years), you need a PCC from each country. Managing this requirement involves:

  1. Identifying all relevant countries. List every country where you resided for more than 6-12 months (thresholds vary by UAE authority) during the specified period.
  2. Timing your applications. Since PCCs from different countries have different processing times and validity periods, coordinate your applications so all documents are ready and valid simultaneously.
  3. Attestation coordination. Each PCC needs attestation from its respective country. This may require coordination with family members or agents if you are not physically present in those countries.
  4. Translation of all documents. Each PCC requires separate MOJ-certified translation. We offer package pricing for multiple PCC translations when submitted together.

Clients with complex residence histories often benefit from our VIP Concierge service, which includes personalized guidance on sequencing and timeline management for multi-country PCC requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

These are the questions we answer most often about Police Clearance Certificate translation. If your situation is not covered here, please reach out directly for personalized guidance.

How long is a Police Clearance Certificate valid for UAE applications?

For most UAE immigration purposes including Golden Visa and employment visas, a Police Clearance Certificate is typically valid for 3 to 6 months from the date of issue. However, the exact validity period depends on three factors: the issuing country's stated validity (some countries issue PCCs valid for 12 months), the specific UAE authority reviewing your application (GDRFA, ICP, or specific ministries may have different thresholds), and the time elapsed during your application processing.

GDRFA generally accepts PCCs issued within the last 6 months. For Golden Visa applications through ICP, timelines may be stricter, especially for certain eligibility categories. We recommend obtaining your PCC as close as possible to your planned application date and completing attestation and translation immediately upon receipt. This maximizes your validity window and reduces the risk of expiration during processing.

If your PCC is approaching the end of its validity period, we can discuss options including expedited processing to maximize your remaining time.

Do I need a PCC from every country I have lived in?

Yes, for most UAE visa applications including Golden Visa and employment visas, you need a Police Clearance Certificate from every country where you have resided for an extended period during a specified lookback period. The specific thresholds vary:

  • Residence threshold: Typically 6 to 12 months of continuous residence triggers the PCC requirement. The exact threshold depends on the reviewing authority.
  • Lookback period: Commonly 5 to 10 years, though some applications may require your entire adult residence history.
  • Country of nationality: You almost always need a PCC from your country of nationality, even if you have not resided there recently.

Managing multiple PCCs requires careful planning because each country has different application processes, processing times, and validity periods. We can help you understand which PCCs you need based on your specific visa category and guide you on timing your applications so all documents remain valid when submitted together.

For clients with complex residence histories spanning many countries, our VIP Concierge service includes personalized consultation on sequencing and timeline management.

Can you translate a PCC that is not in English?

Absolutely. We translate Police Clearance Certificates from virtually any language into Arabic for UAE official use. Our network of MOJ-licensed translators covers all major world languages including:

  • European languages: French, German, Spanish, Portuguese, Italian, Dutch, Russian, Polish, and others
  • Asian languages: Chinese (Mandarin and Cantonese), Hindi, Urdu, Tamil, Tagalog, Thai, Vietnamese, Japanese, Korean
  • Middle Eastern languages: Arabic (for English translation), Farsi, Turkish, Hebrew
  • African languages: Swahili, Amharic, and others as required

For non-English PCCs, we may use a two-step translation process: first translating from the original language to English, then from English to Arabic. This approach is sometimes required by UAE authorities who want both English and Arabic versions. We explain the process and any cost implications before you commit.

Some countries issue bilingual PCCs (such as French-English or Spanish-English). In these cases, we work from the most complete version and ensure all content is captured in the Arabic translation.

What attestation is required for a foreign PCC in the UAE?

For UAE government acceptance, a foreign Police Clearance Certificate typically requires attestation through a multi-step chain. The general pattern is:

  1. Home country authentication: Depending on the country, this may include notarization, authentication by the issuing authority, or state-level certification.
  2. Ministry of Foreign Affairs attestation: Your home country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (or equivalent) certifies the document for international use.
  3. UAE Embassy attestation: The UAE Embassy or Consulate in your home country provides their attestation. This must be done before arriving in the UAE.
  4. UAE MOFA/MOFAIC attestation: Upon arrival in the UAE (or through the MOFAIC app), the document receives final attestation from the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Hague Apostille note: If your country is a signatory to the Hague Apostille Convention, an apostille stamp may simplify or replace some of the earlier authentication steps. However, UAE Embassy attestation and UAE MOFA attestation are still typically required.

We review your PCC during our initial assessment and can advise on the specific attestation requirements for your issuing country. If attestation is incomplete, we explain what additional steps are needed before translation can proceed for official use.

What is the difference between PCC and Good Conduct Certificate?

Police Clearance Certificate and Good Conduct Certificate are essentially the same document, though different countries and contexts use different terminology. The purpose is identical: to provide an official statement about your criminal record status for use by foreign authorities.

Here are the common names used by different countries:

  • United States: FBI Identity History Summary (informally called "FBI background check" or "FBI clearance")
  • United Kingdom: ACRO Police Certificate (also called "UK police certificate" or "criminal record check")
  • India: Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)
  • Philippines: NBI Clearance (National Bureau of Investigation)
  • Australia: National Police Check (or AFP National Police Check for federal-level)
  • Canada: RCMP Criminal Record Check
  • South Africa: Police Clearance Certificate or SAPS Clearance
  • Germany: FÃ¼hrungszeugnis (Certificate of Good Conduct)

Regardless of the specific name or format your country uses, we translate all these document types and are familiar with the standard layouts from police authorities worldwide.

Can I get my PCC translated before attestation is complete?

Yes, you can proceed with translation before attestation is complete. Some clients do this to save time when they are confident the attestation will be completed successfully and they want the translation ready the moment attestation is finished.

However, there are important considerations:

  • Missing attestation stamps: If the attestation process adds stamps, stickers, or seals to your document, these will not appear in the translation. For official acceptance, the translation typically needs to reflect the fully attested document, including references to all attestation marks.
  • Re-translation may be needed: If significant attestation stamps are added after translation, you may need to update the translation to include these elements. This could incur additional fees.
  • Authority requirements vary: Some UAE authorities are flexible about the timing, while others strictly require the translation to show all attestation stamps. The level of scrutiny may also depend on the examiner handling your case.

Our recommendation in most cases is to complete attestation first, then proceed with translation. This ensures the translation is complete and ready for submission without further amendments. However, we understand that timing pressures sometimes make parallel processing necessary, and we can accommodate this approach.

During your initial document review, we assess your attestation status and advise on the best approach for your specific situation and timeline.
