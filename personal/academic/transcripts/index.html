Understanding Academic Transcript Translation for the UAE

Academic transcripts are among the most detail-dense documents we translate. Unlike a degree certificate that confirms your qualification in a few lines, a transcript lists every course you took, every grade you earned, and often additional information about your academic standing, credits, and institutional policies. Translating this accurately requires both linguistic precision and familiarity with academic terminology across different education systems.

In the UAE, translated transcripts are required for various purposes: university admissions, postgraduate applications, credential evaluation by MOHESR, professional licensing, and employment verification. Each of these uses demands an MOJ-certified Arabic translation that accurately represents your academic record without distortion or interpretation.

The challenge with transcript translation lies in the details. Course titles must be translated to convey their actual content, not just literal word-for-word conversion. Grading scales must be presented clearly so the receiving institution understands your performance. Academic terminology like "credit hours," "GPA," "Dean's List," or "academic probation" must be rendered using the appropriate Arabic equivalents recognized in UAE educational contexts.

We approach each transcript with careful attention to these nuances. Our translators have backgrounds in academic translation and understand how different education systems work. They know that "First Class Honours" from a British university, "Summa Cum Laude" from an American institution, and "Distinction" from an Indian university all represent similar levels of achievement, but each must be translated accurately as it appears on the original document.

When You Need Transcript Translation

There are several common situations where you will need your academic transcript translated into Arabic for UAE authorities or institutions.

University Admission

UAE universities typically require Arabic translations of foreign transcripts as part of undergraduate or postgraduate admission packages. Even if the admissions committee can read English, official university records must include MOJ-certified Arabic translations. This applies to both public universities and private institutions.

MOHESR Credential Evaluation

The Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research evaluates foreign qualifications for degree equivalency. The evaluation package requires your original attested transcript plus an MOJ-certified Arabic translation. MOHESR uses this to verify your academic record and determine how your qualification maps to UAE standards.

Employment Verification

Some employers, particularly government entities and major corporations, require translated transcripts as part of their hiring documentation. This may be for internal records, visa processing, or compliance with sector-specific regulations. The translation proves that your claimed qualifications are genuine.

Professional Licensing

Healthcare professionals, engineers, teachers, and other regulated professions often need translated transcripts when applying for UAE professional licenses. The licensing authority needs to verify that your education meets their requirements, which means reviewing your complete academic record in Arabic.

Scholarship Applications

UAE government scholarships and institutional scholarships often require translated transcripts to evaluate academic performance. The scholarship committee needs to compare applicants from different educational backgrounds, and standardized Arabic translations help facilitate this comparison.

Transfer Credits

Students transferring from foreign universities to UAE institutions need translated transcripts for credit evaluation. The receiving university's registrar reviews your courses to determine which credits can transfer. Accurate course title translation is essential for this process.

What We Translate in Your Transcript

Academic transcripts vary in format and content depending on the issuing institution. We translate every element that appears on your document, ensuring nothing relevant is omitted.

Your full name, student ID number, date of birth, enrollment dates, program of study, major and minor fields, and any other identifying information. Names are transliterated into Arabic following consistent conventions. We check for consistency with other documents you may have translated, such as your degree certificate, to avoid discrepancies that could cause problems.

Every course listed on your transcript is translated with its course code (preserved as-is), course title (translated meaningfully), credit hours or units, and the semester or term in which it was taken. For courses with technical or specialized names, we use appropriate Arabic terminology from the relevant academic field. General education courses, electives, and major requirements are all handled with equal care.

Individual course grades are preserved exactly as they appear, whether letter grades (A, B+, C), numerical scores (85, 72), percentages, or grade points. We do not convert grades to a different scale. Cumulative GPA, semester GPA, and any other aggregate measures are also translated precisely. If your transcript includes a grading scale legend, we translate that as well so the reader understands the context.

Dean's List notations, honor roll designations, Latin honors (Cum Laude, Magna Cum Laude, Summa Cum Laude), class rankings, and any academic distinctions are translated using recognized Arabic equivalents. Academic probation, warnings, or other status notations are also translated accurately. We use terminology that UAE academic institutions will recognize and understand.

The name of the issuing institution, its address, the registrar or official who signed the transcript, official seals and stamps, and any accreditation statements. We translate text within seals and stamps while noting the presence of official markings. The date of issue and any reference numbers are included. If the transcript includes a statement about the institution's accreditation or recognition, this is translated as well.

Many transcripts include a legend explaining the grading scale, credit system, course numbering, and other institutional policies. This information is often printed on the back of the transcript or as a separate page. We translate these explanatory sections completely, as they are essential for understanding the main content of the transcript.

Handling Different Grading Systems

One of the most common concerns clients have is whether their grades will be "converted" or somehow altered in translation. We want to be clear about our approach: we translate grades exactly as they appear on your transcript without converting them to a different scale.

Different education systems use different grading conventions:

System Common Scale Our Approach
American Letter grades (A-F), GPA (0-4.0) Preserved as-is with Arabic labels
British Class system (First, 2:1, 2:2, Third) Translated with recognized Arabic terms
Indian Percentage (0-100), Divisions/Classes Numbers preserved, divisions translated
German 1.0-5.0 (1.0 is highest) Scale preserved with explanatory note
French 0-20 (rarely above 16) Numbers preserved with mention system
Australian HD, D, C, P, F or 7-point GPA Grades translated with Arabic equivalents

We do not provide grade equivalency assessments because that is the role of credential evaluation services like MOHESR. Our job is to present your academic record accurately in Arabic so that the evaluating authority can make their own determination. If your transcript includes a grading scale legend, we translate it to help the reader understand the context of your grades.

The Translation Process

Academic transcripts require meticulous attention to detail. Our process ensures accuracy while delivering within your timeline.

Step 1: Document Submission

Send us a clear scan or photo of your complete transcript via WhatsApp or email. If your transcript has multiple pages or includes a back page with the grading legend, include everything. We need to see the complete document to provide an accurate quote and timeline.

Step 2: Assessment

We review the transcript's length, language, complexity, and any special features. Transcripts with many courses, technical programs, or unusual formats require more time. We provide an honest assessment of timing and pricing before you commit.

Step 3: Translation

Your transcript is assigned to a translator with academic translation experience. They translate each element systematically: student information, course-by-course listings, grades, totals, and institutional details. For specialized programs (engineering, medicine, law), we use translators familiar with that field's terminology.

Step 4: Quality Review

A second translator reviews the completed translation against the original. They verify that every course is accounted for, grades match exactly, and totals are consistent. This dual-review process catches errors that could cause problems with your application.

Step 5: MOJ Certification

The verified translation receives official MOJ certification including the stamp, QR verification code, and translator's license details. This certification makes the translation legally valid for UAE government and institutional use.

Step 6: Delivery

Digital copies are sent immediately upon completion. Physical copies with original stamps are couriered to your location or held for pickup. For urgent university deadlines, we can arrange expedited delivery.

Attestation and Credential Evaluation

Translation is one step in the broader process of having your foreign credentials recognized in the UAE. Understanding the complete process helps you plan your timeline.

Attestation Chain: Before translation, your original transcript typically needs attestation through the standard diplomatic chain: issuing institution verification, home country authority (such as HEC for Pakistan or HRD for India), home country Ministry of Foreign Affairs, UAE Embassy in your home country, and finally UAE MOFA. The attested original is then translated.

MOHESR Evaluation: For degree equivalency, you submit the attested original transcript plus the MOJ-certified translation to MOHESR. They evaluate your qualification and issue an equivalency certificate. This process can take several weeks, so plan accordingly if you have application deadlines.

University Direct Submission: Some universities accept transcripts through specific channels like WES, ECE, or direct institutional verification. Even in these cases, an MOJ-certified Arabic translation is often still required for official registration after admission.

We can advise on the typical process for your specific country and institution. The requirements can vary, so it helps to check with the receiving authority before starting the attestation and translation process.

Transcripts from Different Education Systems

Our experience with transcripts from around the world helps us handle the unique features of each education system.

American Transcripts: Typically include semester-by-semester course listings, credit hours, letter grades, and cumulative GPA. May include transfer credits from other institutions. We are familiar with different formats from state universities, private colleges, and community colleges.

British Transcripts: Often more compact, showing module codes, credits, and marks. May include classification predictions or final degree classification. We understand the nuances of the British degree classification system and translate accordingly.

Indian Transcripts: Vary significantly by university and board. May show marks out of 100, grade points, or divisions. CBSE and state board mark sheets have different formats. Engineering and medical transcripts often include practical marks separately.

Pakistani Transcripts: HEC-attested transcripts follow specific formats. Degree programs show semester-wise marks and CGPAs. We are familiar with the grading systems used by major Pakistani universities.

Philippine Transcripts: Often include detailed course listings with units and grades. May show both numerical grades and equivalent ratings. Multiple semesters are typically shown on continuous pages.

European Transcripts: ECTS credits are common across EU countries. Grading scales vary by country (German 1-5, French 0-20, Italian 0-30). Bologna Process documentation often accompanies transcripts. We handle these variations appropriately.

If your transcript is from an education system not mentioned here, do not worry. We have translated transcripts from over 100 countries and can handle virtually any format.
