UNDERSTANDING DEGREE TRANSLATION

What University Degree Translation in the UAE Actually Involves

Translating a university degree is not simply converting text from one language to another. It is part of a larger credentialing process that determines whether your qualification will be recognized for the purpose you need it — whether that is a Golden Visa application, a job offer, or a professional license.

In the UAE, most official processes require documents in Arabic or accompanied by certified Arabic translations. But before translation even begins, foreign educational credentials typically need to go through an attestation chain — a series of stamps from authorities in your home country and the UAE that verify the document's authenticity.

The specific requirements depend on three factors: (1) where your degree was issued, (2) what you need the translated degree for, and (3) whether your profession requires additional verification. A software engineer applying for Golden Visa has different requirements than a doctor applying for a DHA license, even if both hold degrees from the same country.

We operate as a document coordination desk — we review your degree, identify where it sits in the attestation chain, advise on what steps remain, and handle the translation portion with MOJ-licensed translators. We do not pretend that every situation is simple. Some are straightforward; others involve complexities around multiple nationalities, distance learning programs, or professional bodies with their own verification requirements. We try to give you a realistic picture before you commit time and money.

Our approach is practical rather than promotional. We have seen people spend money on translations that were ultimately rejected because the underlying attestation was incomplete. We have seen people wait months for equivalency certificates they did not actually need. Our goal is to help you avoid those situations by being honest about what your specific case requires.

Before we translate your degree, we need to understand what you need it for. Different purposes have different requirements, and not understanding these distinctions can lead to wasted time and money.

Ministry of Education Equivalency

  • Required for UAE government jobs
  • Required for teaching positions in schools
  • Required for some regulated professions
  • NOT required for most private sector jobs
  • NOT typically required for Golden Visa
  • Process takes 2-4 weeks after attestation
  • Requires fully attested original degree

Equivalency confirms your degree matches UAE educational standards. Many people assume they need it when they do not. We advise based on your specific situation.

Attestation Requirements

  • Notarization/authentication in issuing country
  • Home country Ministry of Foreign Affairs stamp
  • UAE Embassy stamp in your home country
  • UAE MOFA attestation (final step)
  • Apostille countries may have simplified process
  • Some countries require additional steps
  • Attestation must be complete before translation

The attestation chain verifies your degree is genuine. Without complete attestation, UAE authorities will typically reject the translation.

Transcript Requirements

  • Some employers require transcripts with degrees
  • Professional licensing often needs grade details
  • DHA/HAAD require transcripts for healthcare
  • Engineering bodies may need course-by-course review
  • Transcripts need separate attestation
  • Mark sheets and transcripts are different documents
  • We translate both if needed

The degree certificate alone may not be sufficient. We help you identify whether transcripts, mark sheets, or other supporting documents are also required.

MODULE C

After-Care Guide: Using Your Translated Degree

Translation is usually just one step in a larger process. Here is what typically comes next, depending on why you needed the degree translated.

Golden Visa Applications

The Golden Visa program has specific requirements for educational credentials. Here is the typical pathway after receiving your translated degree:

  1. Gather supporting documents. Beyond the degree, you typically need passport copies, professional CV, salary certificates or business ownership proof, and potentially letters from employers or professional bodies.
  2. Submit through ICP or an approved typing center. The application can be submitted online through the ICP (Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Port Security) portal or through authorized service centers.
  3. Await document verification. ICP may verify your credentials through their own channels. Having complete attestation on the original degree typically speeds this process.
  4. Complete medical and Emirates ID steps. Once approved in principle, you will need to complete medical fitness tests and apply for your Emirates ID.
  5. Receive your 10-year Golden Visa. The visa is stamped in your passport or issued as a separate document depending on current procedures.

Timeline varies significantly based on individual circumstances. The translation portion is typically the fastest step; attestation and verification take longer.

Job Applications and Employment

For employment purposes, the translated degree is typically used during the visa and work permit application process:

  1. Submit to HR or PRO. Your employer's HR department or PRO (Public Relations Officer) will collect the translated degree along with other employment documents.
  2. MOHRE labor contract processing. The Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation reviews credentials as part of work permit issuance. Properly attested and translated degrees reduce the chance of delays.
  3. Free zone authority processing. If you are employed in a free zone (DMCC, DIFC, JAFZA, etc.), the free zone authority handles visa processing and may have their own document requirements.
  4. Credential verification. Some employers, particularly in regulated industries, may conduct additional verification through services like DataFlow or direct contact with your university.

Keep both the original attested degree and the translation safe. Some employers require the original for their records, while government portals may only need scanned copies.

Professional Licensing: DHA, HAAD, and Others

Regulated professions in the UAE require licensing from specific authorities. Each has its own process, but translated degrees are almost always required:

  1. Dubai Health Authority (DHA). Healthcare professionals need DHA licensing to practice in Dubai. This requires degree translation, transcript translation, DataFlow verification, and often additional professional certificates.
  2. Department of Health Abu Dhabi (DOH/HAAD). Similar to DHA but for Abu Dhabi. Some requirements differ, and professionals need separate licenses for each emirate where they practice.
  3. RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Agency). Real estate professionals need RERA licensing. Degree requirements depend on the specific role — brokers, developers, and property managers have different requirements.
  4. Engineering bodies. Engineers may need to register with the Society of Engineers or specific free zone authorities. Course-by-course transcript review may be required.
  5. Legal profession. Lawyers face the most complex requirements, including potential bar exams and sponsorship by a local law firm.

Each licensing body has specific requirements. We can advise on what documentation they typically require based on our experience with other clients in your profession.

Document Management and Future Use

Your translated degree is a long-term asset. Here is how to maintain and use it effectively:

  1. Store originals safely. Keep the original attested degree and the stamped translation in a secure location. A home safe or bank deposit box is advisable for irreplaceable documents.
  2. Maintain digital copies. High-resolution scans of both the original and translation should be stored securely — cloud storage with encryption, or on multiple devices. Many applications accept digital submissions.
  3. Translation remains valid. Unlike some legal documents, degree translations do not typically expire. However, if you obtain additional qualifications or if the original degree is re-issued, you will need new translations.
  4. Multiple copies if needed. If you anticipate needing the translation for multiple simultaneous applications (multiple employers, visa and licensing at once), we can provide additional stamped sets at a reduced rate.
  5. Notarization for foreign use. If you later need the UAE translation for use in another country, additional notarization or apostille may be required. We can advise on this if it becomes relevant.

MODULE E

Frequently Asked Questions

These are the questions we answer most often about university degree translation. If your situation is not covered here, please reach out directly.

Do I need degree equivalency before getting my degree translated?

It depends entirely on your purpose. For Golden Visa applications and most private sector employment, you need attestation but not necessarily equivalency. The attestation chain (home country authentication → UAE Embassy → UAE MOFA) verifies your degree is genuine, which is sufficient for most purposes.

Equivalency from the UAE Ministry of Education is a separate process that confirms your degree matches UAE educational standards. This is typically required for government jobs, teaching positions in public schools, and certain regulated professions. The equivalency process takes 2-4 weeks after your degree is fully attested.

We advise you on which path applies to your situation before you spend money on unnecessary steps. Many people waste time and money on equivalency when they do not actually need it for their purpose.

What is the full attestation chain for a university degree in the UAE?

The standard attestation chain for most countries is:

  1. Notarization or authentication in your home country. This may involve the issuing university, a notary public, or a government education department depending on your country's procedures.
  2. Your home country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs. They stamp the document to confirm it is authentic and ready for international use.
  3. UAE Embassy in your home country. The embassy attests that the document has been properly authenticated in the issuing country.
  4. UAE MOFA (Ministry of Foreign Affairs). This is the final attestation step, done in the UAE, confirming the document is ready for use with UAE authorities.

Countries that are party to the Hague Apostille Convention may have a simplified process where an Apostille stamp replaces some of these steps. However, the UAE is not a party to the convention, so UAE Embassy attestation is still typically required.

After attestation is complete, the degree is ready for MOJ-certified translation. We can guide you through the specific requirements for your country of origin.

Can I translate my degree before attestation?

Technically yes, but practically it often does not serve your purpose. Most UAE authorities require the original attested degree plus its translation. The translation includes reference to the attestation stamps, and if those stamps are not present, the translation may be rejected.

If you translate an unattested degree and later complete attestation, you will likely need to pay for translation again once the attestation stamps are added. The stamps change the document, and authorities want to see those stamps reflected in the translation.

There are some exceptions. If you need a translation purely for an employer's internal review before attestation is complete, we can do that with clear notation that attestation is pending. But for official submissions, we recommend waiting until attestation is complete.

I have multiple degrees. Do I need to translate all of them?

Usually, only the highest relevant qualification needs translation and attestation. For Golden Visa, your bachelor's degree or higher is typically sufficient — you do not need to translate your high school diploma or lower qualifications.

For professional licensing, the specific degree required depends on the profession. A doctor needs their medical degree; a nurse needs their nursing qualification. An MBA is not relevant if you are applying for a healthcare license based on your medical degree.

For employment, check with your employer or their PRO about which qualifications they need documented. Some positions may require proof of specific certifications in addition to your degree.

We help you identify which degree(s) are actually needed for your purpose, so you do not overspend on unnecessary translations. If in doubt, send us photos of all your qualifications and explain your purpose — we will advise which ones require translation.

How long does the complete degree attestation and translation process take?

The translation itself takes 24 hours (Standard), 6 hours (Express), or 1 hour (VIP). This is the fastest part of the process.

If your degree is not yet attested, the attestation process varies significantly by country:

  • India, Pakistan, Philippines: 2-4 weeks if documents are already in-country; longer if you need to send documents internationally.
  • UK, US, Canada, Australia: 2-6 weeks depending on university and government processing times.
  • European countries: 2-4 weeks for most; some have faster digital verification systems.
  • Middle East, Africa: Varies widely by country; some have efficient systems, others may take 2-3 months.

If you are in a hurry, starting the attestation process early is critical. The translation can be done quickly once attestation is complete, but attestation itself cannot usually be rushed. We can provide timeline estimates specific to your situation once we know your country and current document status.

Is my degree translation accepted for DHA or HAAD healthcare licensing?

Yes, our MOJ-certified degree translations are accepted by Dubai Health Authority (DHA), Department of Health Abu Dhabi (DOH, formerly HAAD), and other UAE healthcare regulators. We have translated thousands of medical degrees, nursing qualifications, and allied health certifications for professionals seeking UAE licensing.

Healthcare licensing typically requires additional steps beyond translation:

  • Primary Source Verification (PSV) through DataFlow or similar services, which verifies your credentials directly with the issuing institution.
  • Professional experience letters from previous employers, also verified through DataFlow.
  • Good standing certificates from medical councils or nursing boards in countries where you have practiced.
  • Transcripts showing your course subjects and grades, particularly for nursing and allied health professions.

We can translate all these documents and advise on the complete documentation package required for your specific healthcare profession and target authority.

What if my degree is in a language other than English?

We translate degrees from virtually any language directly to Arabic. Our network of MOJ-licensed translators covers all major world languages including French, German, Spanish, Portuguese, Russian, Chinese (Mandarin and Cantonese), Japanese, Korean, Hindi, Urdu, Bengali, Tagalog, and many others.

For some less common languages, or when the target authority requires both English and Arabic versions, we may use a two-step process: original language to English, then English to Arabic. This is sometimes necessary when:

  • The employer specifically requests an English version alongside Arabic.
  • Professional licensing bodies require English documentation.
  • The original language is rare and direct Arabic translation is not available.

We explain the process and any cost implications before you proceed. In most cases, direct translation to Arabic is sufficient and more cost-effective.
