UNDERSTANDING YOUR REQUIREMENTS

Why Birth Certificate Translation in the UAE is Different

A birth certificate is more than a record of when and where someone was born. In the UAE immigration context, it is the primary proof of the parent-child relationship that allows you to sponsor a dependent. When this document is rejected, families face separation, delayed schooling, and missed employment opportunities.

The UAE operates under a legal system where Arabic is the governing language for all official procedures. This means that any foreign-language birth certificate submitted to government authorities must be accompanied by an Arabic translation prepared by a Ministry of Justice licensed legal translator. The translation is not merely a linguistic exercise; it carries legal weight and the translator assumes responsibility for its accuracy.

Birth certificates from different countries come in vastly different formats. Some are simple single-page documents with standardized fields. Others span multiple pages with handwritten entries, official seals, apostille stamps, and notarial certifications. Some countries issue birth certificates in languages that do not use the Latin alphabet, requiring careful transliteration of names that will appear on UAE residence visas and Emirates IDs. A translator who is not familiar with these variations can produce a technically accurate translation that nevertheless causes problems at the typing centre or immigration counter.

Common issues we encounter include inconsistent spelling of names across different documents, dates rendered in formats that UAE systems do not recognize, and missing attestation chains that render the entire translation unusable. By checking these elements before we begin translation, we help you avoid the frustration of discovering problems only when you are standing at a government service centre with an appointment slot you cannot recover.

We focus on getting your specific document through your specific authority on your specific timeline. This is what we mean by the concierge approach.

MODULE A

Compliance Checklist: Prerequisites for Translation

Before we begin translating your birth certificate, certain requirements must be in place depending on how and where you intend to use the document. This checklist helps you understand what is needed and avoid wasted effort.

For most UAE government uses, including GDRFA visa sponsorship and Golden Visa applications, your birth certificate needs to complete what is called the attestation chain. This typically involves the following steps, though specifics vary by country of origin:

Standard Attestation Chain

  • Authentication by your home country's relevant authority (varies by country)
  • Attestation by your home country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  • Attestation by the UAE Embassy or Consulate in your home country
  • Final attestation by UAE MOFA (Ministry of Foreign Affairs)

Hague Convention Countries

  • Apostille stamp from the designated authority in your home country
  • UAE MOFA attestation (even with apostille, this is often required)
  • Not all authorities accept apostille alone; check specific requirements
Important Note

The UAE's MOFA attestation is typically required regardless of whether your country is party to the Hague Convention. The apostille simplifies the home-country authentication step but does not replace UAE MOFA. We can advise you on the specific chain for your country of origin when you share your documents with us.

We have handled thousands of birth certificate translations and have catalogued the most common reasons for rejection at various UAE authorities. Understanding these in advance saves significant time and frustration:

Document-Related Issues

  • Missing attestation stamps or incomplete attestation chain
  • Expired attestation (some authorities require recent attestation)
  • Poor quality scan where text or stamps are not legible
  • Name spelling differs from passport or other submitted documents
  • Date of birth format not matching other documents

Translation-Related Issues

  • Translation not performed by MOJ-licensed translator
  • Missing translator stamp, signature, or license number
  • No QR code for verification (newer requirement)
  • Translation does not include all pages of original document
  • Inconsistent transliteration of names
Our Pre-Validation Service

We specifically check for these issues before we begin translation. If we identify a problem, we will tell you how to resolve it. This may mean obtaining a new copy of the birth certificate, completing missing attestation steps, or clarifying name spellings with the relevant authorities.

Standard birth certificates listing biological parents are straightforward. However, we regularly handle more complex situations that require careful attention:

  • Adoption: Some birth certificates are re-issued after adoption with adoptive parents listed. Others include both biological and adoptive parent information. UAE authorities have specific views on how adoption is recognized, which affects how these documents should be translated and what additional documentation may be needed.
  • Surrogacy arrangements: Birth certificates from surrogacy situations vary significantly by jurisdiction. Some list the intended parents, others list the surrogate. Court orders and other legal documents may be required alongside the birth certificate. We can advise on what authorities typically require in these situations.
  • Hospital-issued vs. civil registry documents: Some countries issue multiple types of birth documentation. Hospital certificates, civil registry extracts, and official birth certificates may all exist for the same child. We help you identify which document carries the authority required for UAE purposes.
  • Reconstructed or replacement certificates: If the original birth certificate was lost or destroyed and a replacement was issued, this may require additional explanation or documentation. We can advise on how to handle these situations.

If your situation does not fit the standard pattern, please share the details with us. We have likely encountered similar cases and can provide practical guidance based on what has worked before.

How We Process Your Birth Certificate Translation

Our process is designed to be transparent at every step. You will know exactly where your document is, what is happening, and when to expect the next update. No black boxes, no waiting and wondering.

Step 1: Digital Scan Review

You send us a photo or scan of your birth certificate via WhatsApp or email. We check for legibility, completeness, and the presence of required attestation stamps. If the image quality is insufficient or something appears to be missing, we will let you know immediately and explain what is needed. This step typically takes 15-30 minutes during working hours.

During this review, we also check that the names and dates on your birth certificate align with your other documents (passport, existing UAE visa, Emirates ID if applicable). Discrepancies discovered at this stage are much easier to address than those discovered at the immigration counter.

Step 2: Legal Translation by MOJ Partner

Once we confirm your document is ready for translation and receive payment confirmation, the birth certificate is assigned to a Ministry of Justice licensed legal translator. The translator is selected based on language pair expertise and familiarity with the issuing country's document formats.

The translation process involves careful rendering of all text including headers, footers, stamps, seals, and any handwritten entries. Names are transliterated following established conventions that align with how they will appear on UAE government documents. Dates are converted to the Gregorian calendar format used in UAE systems while noting any alternative calendar references in the original.

Step 3: Draft Approval via WhatsApp

Before final stamping, we send you a PDF draft of the translation for review. This is your opportunity to check that names are spelled as you expect them to appear on UAE documents, dates are correct, and all information has been captured accurately.

We encourage you to compare the draft against your passport and any existing UAE documents. If you spot a discrepancy or want to discuss how a particular name should be rendered in Arabic, this is the time to raise it. Changes at draft stage cost nothing and take minutes; changes after stamping require new pages and additional time.

Step 4: Final Stamping with QR Code

Once you approve the draft, the translation is finalized and printed on official legal translation paper. The MOJ-licensed translator applies their personal stamp, signature, and license details. A QR code is included that allows authorities to verify the authenticity of the translation.

You receive a scanned copy of the stamped translation immediately via WhatsApp or email. The original stamped hard copy is either made available for collection from our Palm Jumeirah location or sent to you via courier, depending on your preference and location. Courier delivery to Dubai addresses is typically same-day; other Emirates receive next-day delivery.

MODULE C

After-Care Guide: What to Do With Your Translated Birth Certificate

Now that you have your translated birth certificate, here is what typically comes next depending on your situation. This guide covers the most common use cases and the steps involved in each.

Opening a Sponsor File for Child Visa on GDRFA

If you are sponsoring your child as a dependent on your UAE residence visa, the translated birth certificate is one of several documents you will submit through the GDRFA (General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs) process. Here is how the process typically unfolds:

  1. Ensure you meet sponsor eligibility requirements. Most employment visa holders can sponsor dependents, but salary thresholds and accommodation requirements may apply depending on your visa category and emirate.
  2. Gather all required documents. Beyond the birth certificate, you will typically need: your passport and residence visa copies, your child's passport, passport-sized photographs, marriage certificate translation (if applicable), tenancy contract or title deed, and salary certificate from your employer.
  3. Submit the application via the GDRFA website or app. In Dubai, this is done through the ICP smart services or GDRFA app. Other emirates have their own portals. Upload all documents as clear PDF or image files.
  4. Pay the applicable fees. Entry permit fees, residence visa fees, and medical insurance are required. Fees vary by visa duration and category.
  5. Complete medical fitness test. Once the entry permit is approved and your child enters the UAE (or if already inside on a visit visa), a medical fitness test at an approved centre is required.
  6. Emirates ID biometrics. After medical clearance, visit an Emirates ID centre for biometric registration.
  7. Visa stamping. The residence visa is stamped in your child's passport, completing the process.

The entire process from application to visa stamping typically takes 2-4 weeks, though this varies based on your nationality, completeness of documentation, and current processing volumes.

Including a Child in Your Golden Visa Application

Golden Visa holders can sponsor their children up to age 25 (or older for unmarried daughters, or children with special needs regardless of age). The translated birth certificate proves the qualifying relationship. Here is what to expect:

  1. Confirm your Golden Visa category allows dependent inclusion. Most Golden Visa categories (investor, skilled professional, exceptional talent, outstanding student) permit family sponsorship.
  2. Prepare the family package documentation. Each child needs: passport copies, passport photos, translated birth certificate, and marriage certificate translation (to establish the relationship through both parents if applicable).
  3. Submit through the ICP portal. Golden Visa applications and family additions are processed through ICP (Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Port Security). Applications can be started online.
  4. Attend biometrics appointment. After initial approval, you and your child will need to complete biometrics at an ICP service centre.
  5. Medical fitness and Emirates ID. Standard medical tests and Emirates ID registration follow, similar to regular residence visa processes.
  6. Receive the 10-year residence visa. Children included on a Golden Visa receive the same 10-year validity as the principal applicant.

A key advantage of including children on a Golden Visa rather than a standard dependent visa is that the visa remains valid regardless of the sponsor's employment status. If you change jobs, leave employment, or leave the UAE temporarily, your children's visas continue unaffected.

School Enrollment in the UAE

Many schools in the UAE require a translated birth certificate as part of the enrollment documentation. While the translation requirements for schools are generally less stringent than for immigration, having an MOJ-certified translation ensures acceptance by any institution:

  1. Contact your chosen school's admissions office. Requirements vary by school. Some accept English translations from any certified translator; others specifically request Arabic translations.
  2. Prepare the standard enrollment documents. These typically include: passport copies, residence visa copies, previous school records, vaccination records, and photographs.
  3. Attestation may be required. Some schools require birth certificate attestation for the transcript/leaving certificate from previous schools. The birth certificate itself may or may not require attestation depending on the school.
  4. Submit and pay enrollment fees. School admissions processes vary widely. Some require assessment tests; others offer places based on documentation alone.

If you are enrolling a child in a UAE school while also applying for their residence visa, it is efficient to have the birth certificate translated once to a standard that satisfies both requirements.

Other Common Uses for Translated Birth Certificates

Beyond visa applications and school enrollment, translated birth certificates serve various purposes in the UAE:

  1. Bank account opening. Some banks require birth certificates for minor children's accounts or for family banking packages.
  2. Insurance enrollment. Health insurance policies covering dependents may require birth certificate proof of relationship.
  3. Legal proceedings. Family court matters, inheritance cases, and custody disputes often require certified birth certificate translations.
  4. Citizenship applications. For those eligible for UAE citizenship or dual nationality arrangements, birth certificates are foundational documents.
  5. Notarization and further attestation. If you need the birth certificate for use in another country, the UAE translation may need additional notarization or apostille.

Whatever your specific situation, we recommend keeping both the original birth certificate and the certified translation in a safe place. Digital copies should be stored securely as well. These documents have long-term value and may be needed for various purposes throughout your time in the UAE.

Frequently Asked Questions

These are the questions we answer most often. If your situation is not covered here, please reach out directly.

Can I use a photo from my phone for birth certificate translation?

Yes, you can submit a clear photo taken with your phone. The key requirements are: all four corners of the document must be visible, the text must be legible without blur, and any stamps or seals should be clearly captured. Natural daylight or good indoor lighting produces the best results. Avoid shadows across the document and ensure the image is not tilted at an angle.

We will review your submission and let you know immediately if a clearer image is needed before proceeding. There is no charge for this initial review.

Is this birth certificate translation accepted for Golden Visa applications?

Yes. Our MOJ-certified birth certificate translations are accepted by ICP (Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Port Security) for Golden Visa applications. The translation includes the official stamp, QR code verification, and translator license details required for all Golden Visa family sponsorship documentation.

For Golden Visa family inclusion, the birth certificate must typically be attested through the full chain (home country authentication, UAE Embassy, UAE MOFA) before translation. We can advise on your specific requirements based on your country of origin and the document you have.

Do you deliver translated birth certificates to Abu Dhabi?

Yes, we provide next-day courier delivery to Abu Dhabi and all other Emirates including Sharjah, Ajman, Ras Al Khaimah, Fujairah, and Umm Al Quwain. For urgent cases, same-day delivery to Abu Dhabi can be arranged through our express courier partners for an additional fee.

Digital copies of the stamped translation are sent immediately upon completion regardless of your location, so you can begin any online application processes while waiting for the physical copy.

What if my birth certificate is from another country?

We translate birth certificates from virtually any country and language. Our network of MOJ-licensed translators covers all major world languages including French, German, Spanish, Portuguese, Russian, Chinese, Hindi, Urdu, Tagalog, and many others.

For documents in languages other than English, we may use a two-step process (original language to English, then English to Arabic) if the target authority requires both English and Arabic versions. We will explain the process and any cost implications before you proceed.

Documents from most countries need MOFA attestation before translation for UAE government use. We can advise you on the specific attestation chain for your country of origin when you share your documents with us.

Does my birth certificate need MOFA attestation before translation?

It depends on the intended use. For GDRFA visa sponsorship and Golden Visa applications, the birth certificate typically needs to be attested by: (1) your home country's relevant authority, (2) your home country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs, (3) the UAE Embassy in your home country, and (4) UAE MOFA.

However, for some internal uses such as certain school enrollments or private sector requirements, unattested translations may be accepted. We review each case individually and advise you before you spend money on translation that may not serve its intended purpose.

If your document requires attestation and has not yet been attested, we can guide you through the process or connect you with attestation services.

How long does birth certificate translation take?

Standard service delivers within 24 hours. Express service completes in 6 hours with priority handling. VIP Concierge provides 1-hour drafts with immediate agent assignment.

All timelines begin after document review and payment confirmation. Complex multi-page documents or unusual formats may require additional time, which we communicate upfront. Weekend and evening submissions may have slightly longer timelines depending on the service tier selected.

Can you translate a birth certificate with handwritten entries?

Yes, we regularly translate birth certificates with handwritten sections, which are common in older documents or from certain countries. Our translators are experienced in deciphering various handwriting styles and scripts.

If any entry is genuinely illegible, we will note this transparently in the translation (for example, "[illegible entry]" or "[partially visible]") rather than guessing. We will also advise you on how authorities typically handle such situations and whether obtaining a clearer copy or a certified extract might be worthwhile.
