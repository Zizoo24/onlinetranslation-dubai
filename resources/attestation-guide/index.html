UAE Document Attestation Guide 2025

Step-by-step MOFA chain explained. Country-specific requirements, timelines, and common mistakes to avoid.

2-4 Weeks Process All Countries Covered

What is Document Attestation?

Document attestation is the process of verifying the authenticity of documents for use in another country. In the UAE, foreign documents must go through a "chain of attestation" to be recognized by government entities, courts, and immigration authorities. This chain ensures your documents are genuine and legally valid.

The UAE Attestation Chain

1

Notarization (Home Country)

Get your document notarized by a recognized authority in your home country.

Issuing institution Notary public 1-3 days
2

Foreign Ministry / Apostille

Submit to your country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs for apostille (Hague countries) or attestation.

Apostille certificate MFA attestation 3-7 days
3

UAE Embassy Attestation

Get attestation from the UAE Embassy or Consulate in your home country. Required for non-Hague countries.

UAE Embassy Fee varies by country 3-10 days
4

MOFA UAE Attestation

Final attestation by UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs. This is the last step before translation.

Dubai or Abu Dhabi AED 150 per document 1-3 days
5

MOJ-Certified Translation

Arabic translation by MOJ-licensed translator. Required for all non-Arabic documents submitted to UAE authorities.

We handle this step 24-48 hours

Apostille vs. Full Attestation

Hague Convention Countries

If your country is a Hague Convention member, you only need an Apostille (simplified process). Then proceed directly to MOFA UAE.

Examples: UK, USA, Australia, India, France, Germany

Non-Hague Countries

Countries not in the Hague Convention require the full chain: Notary → Foreign Ministry → UAE Embassy → MOFA.

Examples: Pakistan, Bangladesh, Egypt, Syria

Documents That Need Attestation

Educational

  • Degree certificates
  • Diplomas
  • Transcripts
  • Professional licenses
  • Training certificates

Personal

  • Birth certificates
  • Marriage certificates
  • Divorce decrees
  • Death certificates
  • Police clearance

Commercial

  • Trade licenses
  • Power of Attorney
  • Articles of Association
  • Board resolutions
  • Commercial invoices

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Skipping steps: Each step in the chain must be completed in order. You cannot skip the Foreign Ministry and go directly to UAE Embassy.

Translating before attestation: Documents must be fully attested BEFORE translation. Translate the attested version.

Using photocopies: Attestation requires original documents. Photocopies are not accepted at any step.

Expired documents: Some documents (like police clearance) have validity periods. Check requirements before starting.

Pro Tips

Start early: The full attestation chain can take 2-4 weeks. Don't wait until the last minute.

Keep copies: Make copies of each attestation stage for your records.

Use a service: Attestation agents in your home country can handle steps 1-3 for you.

Check validity: Some attested documents have limited validity (6 months to 1 year). Plan accordingly.
